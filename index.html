About me:

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Mathematics at Rutgers University.
I am also a full Professor in the Mathematical Institute at the University of Wrocław.
I was a member of the School of Mathematics at the Institute for Advanced Study in
Princeton in 2016/2017 and 2022/2023. I completed my PhD in Mathematics at the
University of Wrocław in June 2011. I obtained my Habilitation degree from the
University of Bonn in June 2016 and from the University of Wrocław in June 2017.


Research interests:

The primary focus of my research to date has been Fourier analysis and its role in understanding phenomena
of norm and pointwise convergence in analysis, ergodic theory and probability theory. Recently, I have also
become interested in number theory and additive combinatorics from the perspective of Fourier analysis.

ETA($\eta$) Seminar:

I am running an online (ETA($\eta$)=) Ergodic Theory & Analysis Seminar. This is a seminar in ergodic theory and analysis
understood in a broad sense and their applications in additive combinatorics and additive number theory. The ETA($\eta$)
seminar is aimed at a general mathematical audience. Graduate and postdoctoral students are especially welcome
to attend. We meet over Zoom every Wednesday at 11am Eastern Time (New York).


CTM Workshop:

I am organizing a workshop Current Trends in Mathematics: Beyond the Freshmen Horizons. This workshop is perfect
for undergraduate students who plan to further their education in graduate school, as well as graduate students who are
interested in pursuing careers in academia in mathematics. The workshop will take place at DIMACS at Rutgers University
from June 10th to June 14th, 2024. If you are interested in attending either online or in-person, please visit the website here.
